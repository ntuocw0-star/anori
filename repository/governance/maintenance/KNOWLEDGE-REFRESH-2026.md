## Rationale

The Repository's core Evidence Governance is mature. The three gaps that remain create user friction:

```
Gap A:  No Guideline-layer evidence
        Users ask "what do doctors recommend?" — Repository has SR/MOAS but no Guideline
        → Clinical credibility gap

Gap B:  No Intervention evidence for key pathways
        Users ask "so what do we do?" — Repository answers "what is associated"
        → Clinical utility gap (GAP-004 is a symptom of this)

Gap C:  No 2024–2026 publications
        Repository looks static — contradicts "持续更新" identity
        → Trust and return signal gap (PR-000001 addresses surface; this addresses depth)
```

## New Evidence Articles

### EA-KR-001

**Reference:** Ares-Brage, L., Bagur-Calafat, C., Amor-Barbosa, M., Lidón-Moyano, C., & Romero-Galisteo, R. P. (2025). Family-centered care for children with cerebral palsy: A meta-analysis of perspectives from children, caregivers, and professionals through the Measure of Processes of Care. *Disability and Rehabilitation.*

**Design:** Meta-analysis · Measure of Processes of Care (MPOC)
**Population:** Children with CP · caregivers · professionals
**Key finding:** Family-centered care practices — specifically information sharing, coordinated care, and respectful partnering — are associated with better participation outcomes and higher family quality of care ratings. Effect sizes vary by MPOC subscale; "Providing General Information" and "Enabling & Partnership" subscales show strongest association with positive family outcomes.

**Repository role:**
- **Guideline/Practice Evidence** for Participation Hub and Family Support Hub
- First meta-analytic evidence on Family-Centered Care process → participation/FQOL outcomes
- Fills Guideline Gap (Gap A) for CP population

**ET connection:**
- ET-000031 (FS × Participation): FCC practices operationalise "Family Support"
- ET-000037 (FS × FQOL): FCC enabling practices positively associated with FQOL
- KA-FQOL-001: Confirms "Disability-Related Support" domain of Beach Center FQOL as intervention target

**Evidence Level contribution:** Moderate → **Evidence Level for FCC Practice remains Moderate** (meta-analysis, but MPOC is process measure, not participation outcome directly)

**Known Boundary:**
- MPOC measures perceived FCC quality, not participation directly
- CP population; transferability to ASD/ID uncertain
- Cross-sectional perspectives meta-analysis; not RCT

### EA-KR-002

**Reference:** McCarthy, E., & Guerin, S. (2022). Family-centred care in early intervention: A systematic review of the processes and outcomes of family-centred care and impacting factors. *Child: Care, Health and Development, 48*(1), 1–32.

**Design:** Systematic Review
**Population:** Families in early intervention (various disability types, 0–6 years primarily)
**Key finding:** Family-centred care consistently associated with: (1) higher parental self-efficacy, (2) improved family functioning, (3) better emotional well-being outcomes. Key enablers: consistent professional-family partnership, information sharing, family goal-setting involvement. Key barrier: service fragmentation and lack of FCC training in professionals.

**Repository role:**
- **Guideline/Practice SR** covering all three Hub concepts
- Rare SR that directly connects FCC practices to Family Functioning AND FQOL outcomes
- Partially fills GAP-004: FCC → Family Functioning improvement is documented (though not the specific FF → Participation pathway)

**ET connection:**
- ET-000033 (FF × Participation): FCC improves FF (Section 5 update)
- ET-000036 (FS × FF): FCC practice = the mechanism through which FS improves FF
- ET-000037 (FS × FQOL): FCC → Emotional Well-being → FQOL pathway documented

**Evidence Level contribution:** Adds SR-level support for FCC process → FF improvement
**ET-000033 update:** Intervention Evidence Status updates from "Field-level Gap (no RCT)" to "Indirect: FCC interventions improve Family Functioning (McCarthy & Guerin 2022 SR), but direct FF → Participation RCT remains absent"

**Known Boundary:**
- FCC is a practice model, not a specific intervention protocol
- Early intervention focus (0–6); school-age evidence less covered
- Outcome heterogeneity across studies

### EA-KR-003

**Reference:** Kuo, Y., Fettig, A., Zhong, Z.-M., & Garfias, Y. (2025). Exploring Family Quality of Life in Parent-Implemented Interventions for Young Children with Disabilities. *Topics in Early Childhood Special Education.*

**Design:** Systematic Review (40 studies)
**Population:** Young children with disabilities; parents as intervention agents
**Key finding:** Parent-Implemented Interventions (PIIs) show positive trends for:
- Emotional well-being domain of FQOL (most consistent)
- Family Interaction domain
- Parenting domain

Least-addressed domains: Physical well-being and Disability-Related Support.
PIIs that explicitly embed family goals (not just child goals) show stronger FQOL effects.

**Repository role:**
- **Intervention Evidence** for FQOL Hub
- First SR to specifically analyse FQOL as outcome of parent-focused interventions
- Partially fills GAP-004 (indirect path: Parenting intervention → FF improvement → FQOL)
- Fills Intervention Gap (Gap B) for FQOL concept

**ET connection:**
- ET-000037 (FS × FQOL): PIIs with family support component → FQOL improvement
- KA-FQOL-001: Confirms Emotional Well-being as most accessible FQOL domain for intervention
- ET-000033 (FF × Participation): PIIs improve Family Interaction — indirect evidence for FF → participation pathway

**Evidence Level contribution:**
- ET-000037: Adds Intervention Evidence (Indirect) — MOAS remains primary evidence; PII SR adds directional intervention support
- ET-000037 Clinical Utility: Medium → **High** (now has SR-level intervention direction)

**Known Boundary:**
- SR, not MOAS — heterogeneous studies
- PIIs vary widely in content and intensity
- FQOL not always primary outcome in included studies
- Early childhood focus (birth to 5)

### EA-KR-004

**Reference:** Ragni, B., Boldrini, F., Mangialavori, S., Cacioppo, M., Capurso, M., & De Stasio, S. (2022). The efficacy of parent training interventions with parents of children with developmental disabilities. *International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health, 19*(15), 9685.

**Design:** Systematic Review of parent training interventions
**Population:** Parents of children with developmental disabilities (ASD, ID, ADHD)
**Key finding:** Parent training interventions consistently reduce parenting stress (PSI) and improve parenting practices. Effect sizes moderate to large for stress reduction. Most effective formats: structured cognitive-behavioural, group-based, ≥8 sessions. Participation outcomes not directly measured — child adaptive behaviour (proxy) improves.

**Repository role:**
- **Intervention Evidence** for Parenting Stress concept
- First intervention SR explicitly covering PS reduction in DD families added to Repository
- Fills Intervention Gap (Gap B) for Parenting Stress pathway

**ET connection:**
- ET-000032 (PS × Participation): Parent training → ↓PS → ↑Participation (indirect pathway established)
- ET-000035 (FS × PS): Parent training operationalises a specific form of Family Support → PS reduction
- Clinical Navigation in ET-000032 updated: "Parent training interventions have SR-level evidence for PS reduction; participation improvement assumed via ET-000032 association"

**Evidence Level contribution:**
- ET-000032 Intervention Evidence: Now **Indirect** (PT → ↓PS established; ↓PS → ↑Part established separately)
- This is not a direct RCT of PS intervention → participation as outcome, but the two-step pathway is now both supported by SR-level evidence

**Known Boundary:**
- Parent training SR, not participation-outcome SR
- Participation improvement inferred, not directly measured
- Format heterogeneity limits specific recommendations

### EA-KR-005

**Reference:** Dionísio Mestre, T., Lopes, M. J., Matias Mestre, D., Ferrinho Ferreira, R., Pedro Costa, A., & Valente Caldeira, E. (2024). Impact of family-centered care in families with children with intellectual disability: A systematic review. *Heliyon, 10*(7), e28241.

**Design:** Systematic Review (10 studies, 2018–2023)
**Population:** Families of children with intellectual disability
**Key finding:** Nine FCC components identified. Family-centred interventions associated with: improved child outcomes (participation in community/extracurricular activities), higher care plan quality, better family emotional well-being. Shared decision-making component shows strongest association with positive family outcomes.

**Repository role:**
- **2024 Publication** — most recent SR in Repository (fills Gap C)
- Direct evidence for FCC → child participation in community activities
- ID population specifically (gap in current evidence base)
- SDM component = direct validation of Block 4 / SDM design in Template v2.1

**ET connection:**
- ET-000033 (FF × Participation): FCC improves family functioning AND child participation (most direct evidence yet for this pathway)
- ET-000031 (FS × Participation): FCC practices = operationalised Family Support
- ET-000037 (FS × FQOL): FCC → emotional well-being improvement = FQOL Emotional domain

**Evidence Level contribution:**
- ET-000033 update: Clinical Navigation strengthened — FCC is the evidence-based mechanism through which FF and FS improvements may translate to participation
- ID population now has dedicated intervention evidence (closes partial scope gap for ID families)

**Known Boundary:**
- 10 studies; heterogeneous designs
- FCC as practice model, not specific protocol
- SR does not separate FF improvement from participation improvement causally

## ET Updates Triggered by Knowledge Refresh

### ET-000032 (PS × Participation) — Intervention Evidence update

```
Previous:  Intervention Evidence: Field-level Gap

Updated:   Intervention Evidence: Indirect (Sprint 37)
           Parent training interventions have SR-level evidence
           for reducing parenting stress (Ragni et al. 2022).
           Combined with ET-000032's established PS → Participation
           association, this creates a two-step evidence-supported
           intervention pathway:
             Parent Training → ↓ Parenting Stress
             ↓ Parenting Stress → ↑ Participation (ET-000032)
           
           No direct RCT of "Parent Training → Participation"
           as primary outcome exists. Indirect pathway only.
           
           Clinical Navigation addition:
           "For families where PS is the primary concern,
            parent training interventions (group-based, ≥8 sessions,
            CBT-informed) have the strongest intervention evidence
            for PS reduction. See EA-KR-004."
```

### ET-000033 (FF × Participation) — Intervention Evidence update

```
Previous:  Intervention Evidence: Field-level Gap

Updated:   Intervention Evidence: Indirect (Sprint 37)
           Family-centred care (FCC) interventions — particularly
           those incorporating shared decision-making, information
           sharing, and family goal engagement — are associated
           with both improved family functioning AND child participation
           in community activities (McCarthy & Guerin 2022;
           Dionísio Mestre et al. 2024).
           
           This does not establish direct FF → Participation
           intervention causality, but provides practice guidance:
           FCC is the evidence-consistent intervention approach
           when FF and participation are both concerns.
           
           Field-level Gap remains for direct RCT:
           FF-targeted intervention → Participation as primary outcome.
```

### ET-000037 (FS × FQOL) — Intervention Evidence update

```
Previous:  Intervention Evidence: Absent

Updated:   Intervention Evidence: Indirect (Sprint 37)
           Parent-Implemented Interventions with family support
           components → positive trends in FQOL Emotional Well-being

           Clinical Utility: Medium → High (Sprint 37)
```

## Clinical Navigation Updates (summary)

Clinicians can now answer "so what do we do?" with:

```
For PS reduction:
  Parent training (group, CBT-informed, ≥8 sessions) — SR evidence (EA-KR-004)

For FF + Participation:
  Family-centred care practices (shared decision-making, information, partnership)
  — SR + meta-analysis evidence (EA-KR-001, 002, 005)

For FQOL improvement:
  Parent-Implemented Interventions with explicit family goals
  — SR evidence, emotional well-being domain most responsive (EA-KR-003)
```

These three answers fill the most frequent "what do I do?" gap identified across 75 pilot cases.
