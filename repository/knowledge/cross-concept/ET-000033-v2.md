## 5. Intervention Evidence Status (NEW — Sprint 32)

**Status: Field-level Evidence Gap**

No RCT or quasi-experimental study has directly tested the pathway:
> *Intervention targeting Family Functioning → Child Participation improves*

**Why this is a Field-level Gap, not a Repository Coverage Gap:**

Arakelyan et al. (2019) — the canonical SR on family factors and participation (30 studies) — explicitly categorises family factors as either non-modifiable *status factors* or modifiable *process factors*. Family Functioning (internal family structure and interaction patterns, as measured by FAD/FACES) does not appear among the six consistently identified **modifiable process factors**:

```
Six modifiable process factors (Arakelyan 2019):
  1. Parental mental and physical health functioning
  2. Parental self-efficacy beliefs
  3. Parental support
  4. Parental time
  5. Family preferences
  6. Activity orientation
```

Family Functioning maps more closely to a **background structural characteristic** than to a primary intervention target. The SR recommendation is to focus rehabilitation efforts on the modifiable process factors above.

**Clinical implication:** The absence of intervention evidence here is not a gap to fill by finding a clever study — it reflects the field's current theoretical consensus on where intervention effort is most warranted.

## 6. Clinical Navigation (NEW — Sprint 32)

> This section is added to all Cross-concept ETs where evidence is Limited or where Intervention Evidence is absent. It redirects clinical users to the most actionable next step.

**When the evidence here is insufficient for your clinical decision, consider:**

| Priority | Topic | ET | Evidence Level | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Parenting Stress × Participation | ET-000032 | Moderate + Temporal | Strongest modifiable factor; longitudinal evidence; most direct intervention target |
| **2** | Family Support × Participation | ET-000031 | Moderate | Consistent association; SR-level evidence; actionable via support network strengthening |
| **3** | Family Support × Family Functioning | ET-000036 | Limited | If Family Functioning is specifically the concern, this ET shows FS can influence FF |

**Clinical reasoning:**

If a family presents with both low participation and poor family functioning, the evidence-based approach is not to target family functioning directly, but to address parenting stress and family support first — these are the modifiable factors with stronger evidence, and improvements in these areas may indirectly improve family functioning as a downstream effect.

**If family functioning is critically impaired** (e.g., conflict, communication breakdown) and feels like the most urgent presenting issue, consider referral to family therapy — which sits outside the scope of this Repository but addresses FF as a primary target in its own right.
