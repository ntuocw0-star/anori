## 4. Key Findings

### 4.1 Status Factors (Non-modifiable)

Four status factors consistently associated with participation:
- Parental ethnicity
- Parental education
- Family type
- Family socio-economic status

### 4.2 Process Factors (Modifiable) — including Family Support

Six modifiable process factors with consistent associations with participation:
- Parental mental and physical health functioning
- Parental self-efficacy beliefs
- **Parental support** ← Family Support operationalization
- Parental time
- Family preferences
- Activity orientation

Parental Support was consistently positively associated with participation across multiple included studies.

### 4.3 Implications

Process factors, including Parental Support, are more amenable to intervention than status factors. This distinction has direct relevance for family-centered intervention design.

---

## 5. Methodological Notes

- Systematic Review of observational studies; does not support causal or temporal inference
- Parental Support identified as one of six Process Factors; relative contribution vs. other factors not separated
- Majority of included studies examined leisure and recreational participation; school participation evidence limited
- Prospective studies explicitly recommended by authors to confirm findings (Arakelyan et al., 2019, p.521)
